Latest News in Cape Town

    City Developments

  • Cape Town announces new infrastructure projects to improve transportation and public spaces.
    Plans include upgrades to MyCiTi bus routes and revitalization of public squares.

  • New eco-friendly initiatives launched to promote sustainability in the city.
    Solar-powered streetlights and expanded recycling programs are being rolled out in several suburbs.

  • Water-saving campaigns continue to encourage residents to use resources responsibly.
    The city highlights innovative solutions like greywater systems and rainwater harvesting.

    • Events and Festivals

  • The Cape Town International Jazz Festival returns with an exciting lineup of local and international artists.
    Performances will take place at the Cape Town International Convention Centre.

  • Cape Town hosts its annual Food and Wine Festival, showcasing the best of local cuisine and beverages.
    Visitors can enjoy tastings, cooking demonstrations, and live entertainment.

  • Summer outdoor markets bring together local artisans, musicians, and food vendors.
    Popular spots include Greenmarket Square and the Oranjezicht City Farm Market.

    • Community News

  • Local community groups organize beach clean-up events to protect the coastal environment.
    Volunteers gather monthly at Muizenberg and Camps Bay beaches.

  • Cape Town's art scene thrives with new galleries and exhibitions opening across the city.
    Recent highlights include contemporary African art showcases in Woodstock and Salt River.

  • Youth programs are expanding to provide skills training and mentorship.
    Initiatives focus on coding, entrepreneurship, and creative arts for high school students.
